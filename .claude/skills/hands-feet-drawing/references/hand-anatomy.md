## 1. Manga vs Realism: Key Differences

| Feature | Realistic Adult | 14yo Manga Girl |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|
| MCP knuckles | Sharp, prominent | Soft bumps, often omitted |
| PIP/DIP creases | Distinct lines | Faint or absent |
| Palmar creases | 3 clear lines | 0–1 subtle line |
| Finger shape | Bony, angular | Tapered, rounded, "mochi" |
| Nail size | Wide, prominent | Small, oval, pushed back |
| Extensor tendons | Visible ridges | Not shown |
| Veins | Visible dorsally | Never shown |
| Overall feel | Anatomical, structural | Soft, cute, delicate |

**Core principle:** In manga style, less is more. Every bone/crease you omit makes the hand look younger and softer. Every line you add makes it look older.

## 2. Bone Structure (Simplified)

### What to KNOW (even if you don't draw it):

- **27 bones** total: 8 carpals, 5 metacarpals, 14 phalanges
- Thumb = 2 phalanges; fingers 2–5 = 3 each
- The metacarpals fan from the carpal block — 3rd MC is longest

### What to SHOW in manga:

- **Wrist connection:** A smooth oval transition from forearm to palm (no visible styloid processes in most manga).
- **MCP line:** A subtle arc indicating knuckle row — shown as a faint curved line or not at all.
- **Finger segments:** Draw the OUTLINE of each finger. The internal bone structure is implied by the silhouette — do NOT draw bones inside fingers in manga.
- **Thumb CMC:** The base of the thumb is slightly thicker than the thumb shaft, shown as a gentle curve in the palm outline.

## 3. Joint Mechanics (14yo)

| Joint | Adult Visibility | 14yo Manga Visibility |
|-------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Thumb CMC | Subtle | Hidden in palm contour |
| MCP (all) | Prominent bumps | Very subtle curves, often hidden |
| PIP | Distinct crease | Faint line, or merged into finger curve |
| DIP | Distinct crease | Same as PIP — often omitted entirely |
| Thumb IP | Visible crease | Faint or omitted |

**Manga simplification:** Instead of 3 joints per finger, draw 1–2 gentle "bend" lines. The silhouette already communicates the finger shape — creases are unnecessary.

**Knuckle arc:** When drawing the hand dorsum, the MCPs form a soft arc (middle highest). But in many manga styles, this arc is flattened to almost-straight for simplicity.

## 4. Soft Tissue & Surface (Manga Rendering)

### Dorsal (Back of Hand)

**Adult:** Visible knuckles, extensor tendon ridges, dorsal veins
**14yo Manga:** Smooth, soft surface. Only the MCP bumps are subtly indicated (if at all). One or two faint knuckle lines on index and middle fingers at most. No veins, no tendons.

### Palmar (Palm)

**Adult:** Thenar + hypothenar eminences distinct, three deep creases
**14yo Manga:** Palm is a simple rounded pentagon. At most ONE faint crease across the center. Thenar eminence (thumb pad) is softly rounded — shown as a slight outward curve. Hypothenar eminence is barely visible.

### Interdigital Webbing

Shown as **gentle concave arcs** between fingers. In manga:
- Index–middle web: deepest (reaches about 1/3 of the way to PIP)
- Middle–ring, ring–pinky: slightly shallower
- Often drawn as a single soft arc connecting all four finger bases

### Fingernails

**Critical manga detail:**
- Size: Small, about 1/3 of the distal phalanx width
- Shape: Oval/almond, never square
- Position: Set back from the fingertip by ~1mm — NOT at the very fingertip edge
- Highlight: In colored/rendered manga, each nail gets a small white gloss dot
- Thumbnail is slightly wider (still oval)
- Pinky nail is smallest, very delicate

### Fingertip Pads

- Round, plump, soft-looking
- In manga, the pad is drawn as a gentle outward curve at the fingertip
- Often shaded with a subtle pink blush in colored art

## 5. Measurement Landmarks

| Measurement | 14yo Manga Girl |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Hand length (wrist to middle fingertip) | ~3/4 head height |
| Palm length (wrist to MCP of MF) | ~1/2 hand length |
| Palm width (across MCPs) | ~2/3 palm length |
| Finger width at MCP base | ~scale × 0.12 (vs 0.14 adult) |
| Finger width at tip | ~scale × 0.06 (vs 0.07 adult) |
| Middle finger length (MCP to tip) | ~1/2 hand length |
| Index finger | ~93% of middle finger |
| Ring finger | ~90% of middle finger |
| Pinky finger | ~75% of middle finger |
| Nail length | ~30% of distal phalanx |
| Nail width | ~60% of fingertip width |
